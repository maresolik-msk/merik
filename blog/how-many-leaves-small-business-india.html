How many leaves should a small business offer in India?

A practical benchmark for annual, sick and casual leave — and how to write a policy employees actually understand.

Published 12 July 2026 · Merik team · 6 min read

There's no single legal number that applies to every business in India — leave entitlements are shaped by state-specific rules and company policy. This isn't legal advice, but here's a practical way to think about it. Confirm minimums with a compliance advisor for your state.

The common leave types

  • Annual / earned leave — accrued over the year, usually the largest bucket.
  • Casual leave — short, often unplanned days off.
  • Sick leave — for illness, sometimes requiring a note past a certain number of days.

Many small businesses land around 12–18 total paid leave days a year across these categories — but state rules and industry norms both shift this number, so treat it as a starting point, not a rule.

What matters more than the exact number

1. Write the policy down

Ambiguity — not the number itself — is what causes disputes. A written policy that says exactly how many days of each type, and how carry-forward works, prevents most leave arguments before they start.

2. Decide carry-forward and encashment rules upfront

Will unused leave roll over, expire, or be paid out? Pick one and apply it consistently every year-end.

3. Separate leave type from payroll impact

Whatever the entitlement, keep the paid-vs-unpaid distinction clean — see paid vs unpaid leave for how that connects to pay.

A generous policy that's inconsistently applied feels worse to employees than a modest policy applied fairly every time.
